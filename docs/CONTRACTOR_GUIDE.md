## Introduction

LedgerX is the expense and invoice tracking system used by your property manager or admin. As a contractor, you use it for two things:

1. **Submitting receipts** — proof of purchases you made on behalf of a property (materials, supplies, gas, etc.). These are logged as *transactions*.
2. **Submitting invoices** — your own bills for the work you performed. These go through a review cycle: Pending → Approved → Paid (or Rejected with an explanation).

Everything you send through LedgerX goes straight to the admin responsible for the property. You don't email anyone, you don't attach files to texts, you don't lose a receipt in your glove box. You open the app, upload a photo or PDF, and the app reads most of the details for you. Your only job is to double-check what it read.

## The 30-Second Version

1. Go to **ledger.phillyshah.com**, sign in with your username and password.
2. Tap **Add Transaction** for a receipt (something you bought) or **Submit Invoice** for a bill (for work you did).
3. Upload the photo or PDF. The app reads it for you. Fix anything it got wrong.
4. Pick the property, tap **Save** or **Submit Invoice**.
5. Come back anytime to see if your invoice has been **Approved** or **Paid**, or if a receipt has been marked as paid.

That's it. The rest of this guide is the detail for when something isn't obvious.

## Signing In

1. Open a browser (phone or computer) and go to **ledger.phillyshah.com**.
2. Enter the **username** and **password** your admin gave you.
3. Tap **Sign In**.

> **Note:** LedgerX uses a username — not an email — to sign in. You don't need to remember an email address.

**Forgot your password?**

- If you previously added an email in **Settings**, tap **Forgot password?** on the sign-in screen and a reset link will be emailed to you.
- If you haven't added an email yet, message your admin and ask them to reset it. Next time you sign in, open **Settings** and add an email so you can reset it yourself in the future.

**Tip — add LedgerX to your phone's home screen:**
On iPhone, tap the Share button → **Add to Home Screen**. On Android, tap the three-dot menu → **Install app** or **Add to Home screen**. LedgerX now opens like a regular app.

## Submitting a Receipt (Add Transaction)

Use this when you bought something for a property and need to be reimbursed, or when you want the purchase recorded.

1. From the dashboard, tap **Add Transaction**.
2. Tap the upload area and pick the receipt photo or PDF. (You can take a photo right there on your phone.)
3. **LedgerX scans the receipt automatically.** Within a few seconds, it fills in:
   - **Vendor** — where you bought it (Home Depot, Lowe's, etc.)
   - **Amount** — total you paid
   - **Date** — when you bought it
   - **Category** — type of expense (if it recognizes it)
   - **Tax, tip, payment method, itemized summary** — added to the notes if visible
4. Review the filled-in fields. **Fix anything the scan got wrong.** OCR is close, but receipts with faded ink, bad lighting, or unusual layouts can trip it up.
5. Pick the **property** (household) this receipt belongs to.
6. Optionally add **Notes** — anything the admin should know.
7. Tap **Save**.

Your receipt now appears in **My Receipts**. The admin will see it in the system.

**Multiple receipts for one purchase?** You can attach as many files as you want to a single transaction — useful for long itemized receipts that span multiple pages, or when you have both the receipt and a packing slip. The first file is the one the OCR reads.

## Submitting an Invoice

Use this for your *own* invoice — the bill you send for work you performed.

1. From the dashboard, tap **Submit Invoice**.
2. Upload your invoice. **PDFs work, JPG/PNG photos work.** If you generate invoices in Word or Google Docs, export to PDF and upload that.
3. **LedgerX reads the invoice and fills in fields:**
   - **Invoice Number** (optional — leave blank if your invoice doesn't have one)
   - **Amount**
   - **Currency**
   - **Description** — what the work was
   - **Service period start and end** — the dates you performed the work
4. Review the fields and correct anything that's wrong.
5. Pick the **property** where the work was performed.
6. Tap **Submit Invoice**.

Your invoice now appears in **My Invoices** with a yellow **Pending** badge. The admin will review it.

### What happens next

Your invoice moves through three possible states after submission:

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 🟡 **Pending** | Submitted — waiting for the admin to review |
| 🔵 **Approved** | The admin reviewed it and approved it. Payment is being arranged. |
| 🟢 **Paid** | Payment has been issued. You're done. |
| 🔴 **Rejected** | The admin rejected it. A note explains why — shown in an amber box right on the invoice card. |

**If rejected:** read the note, fix whatever was wrong (wrong property, unclear invoice, missing detail, etc.), and submit a new invoice. The old rejected one stays in your list so you have a record.

## Tips for Better OCR

The app uses AI to read your receipts and invoices. It's good, but it's not perfect. Here's how to help it:

- **Good lighting.** Avoid glare, shadows, and heavy reflections. Daylight or a bright indoor light is ideal.
- **Flat paper.** Smooth out crumpled receipts before photographing them.
- **Straight-on angle.** Tilted photos are harder to read. Keep the camera parallel to the paper.
- **Thermal receipts fade fast.** If you have a thermal-paper receipt (the shiny kind from gas stations and stores), scan or photograph it the same day. They turn blank in weeks.
- **One file per scan.** LedgerX reads the *first* file you upload. If you attach a blurry photo first and a clear one second, it'll use the blurry one. Upload the clearest copy first.
- **Invoices from Word/Docs → export PDF.** Don't screenshot a Word document; export it as a PDF and upload that. The text stays sharp.
- **Handwritten notes are fine.** The OCR will try to pick them up and put them in the notes field — review and tidy them up.

## FAQ & Troubleshooting

**Q: I forgot my password.**
A: If you added an email, tap **Forgot password?** on the sign-in screen. If not, ask your admin to reset it — then add an email so you can reset it yourself next time.

**Q: I don't see a property in the dropdown.**
A: Your admin needs to assign you to that property. Message them.

**Q: My photo won't upload.**
A: Supported formats are JPG, PNG, WebP, and PDF. Very large files (over ~10 MB) may time out — try a slightly smaller photo. Password-protected PDFs won't work.

**Q: The OCR got a field wrong.**
A: Just fix it before saving. It's totally normal — no receipt is perfectly readable every time.

**Q: I accidentally submitted an invoice with the wrong details.**
A: Contractors can't edit or delete submitted invoices (to keep the audit trail clean). Message your admin and ask them to reject the invoice with a note explaining what happened; then submit a corrected one.

**Q: I accidentally deleted a receipt.**
A: Deletions are permanent — there's no undo. Be careful when you tap **Delete**.

**Q: My invoice says Rejected. What do I do?**
A: Read the note (shown in an amber box on the invoice card) — it explains why. Fix the issue and submit a fresh invoice. The rejected one stays in your history.

**Q: Can I tell whether an invoice has been paid without opening the app?**
A: Not right now — the app doesn't email or text you. Check the app periodically. We may add notifications later.

**Q: My phone doesn't have a camera app that exports PDF. How do I submit an invoice?**
A: Take a photo of the invoice with your normal camera and upload the JPG — that works fine. PDFs are preferred but not required.

**Q: I submitted the same invoice twice.**
A: Ask the admin to reject the duplicate. Don't worry — it happens.

**Q: Can I use this on a computer as well as my phone?**
A: Yes. Same URL, same login, same features. The layout adjusts for the screen size.
